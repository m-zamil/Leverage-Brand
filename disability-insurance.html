What is Disability Insurance?

Life is unpredictable. Disability insurance is a safeguard for your income. Imagine an unexpected accident or illness prevents you from working; this insurance steps in, providing you with a percentage of your usual earnings. It's more than just a policy—it's peace of mind. With disability insurance, you can ensure that life's unforeseen events don't derail your financial stability, allowing you to focus on recovery without the burden of financial stress. At Leverage, we believe that understanding the essence of this protection is the first step towards a secure future.

What's Protected by Disability Insurance?

Disability insurance replaces up to 60% of your gross income if an illness or injury prevents you from working in your current job, or any other job, depending on your policy details.

How Much Disability Insurance Do I Need?

Leverage’s Insurance Experts usually recommend protecting 60% of your gross pay. This is typically close to you actual take-home pay

What is the Cost of Disability Insurance?

At Leverage, we understand that when it comes to your financial security, knowledge is power. Disability insurance offers peace of mind and protection, but its cost can vary based on several factors. Let's demystify the elements that influence your premium rates:

  1. Age:

    Time Ticks for Everyone
    The younger you are when purchasing a policy, the lower your premiums typically will be. As you age, the likelihood of you becoming disabled increases, and insurers account for this risk.

    Typically, the younger you are when you buy LTC insurance, the lower the premium. As age advances, insurers often associate higher risks, which can increase the cost.

  2. Occupation:

    What's in a Day's Work?
    Jobs with higher risks of injury or strain (e.g., construction worker) may have higher premiums than those in low-risk environments (e.g., office workers).

  3. Income:

    Your Earning Power
    Your current income often dictates the benefit amount you’d require. The higher the desired coverage (aligned with your income), the higher the premium.

  4. Benefit Period:

    How Long Can You Wait?
    Choosing a longer benefit period (like until retirement age) means higher premiums than opting for a shorter duration (like two years).

  5. Waiting or Elimination Period:

    Pause Before Protection
    This is the time you wait before benefits kick in post-disability. A shorter waiting period (like 30 days) will likely cost more than a longer one (like 90 days).

  6. Health History:

    Your Personal Story
    Individuals with a history of chronic illnesses or frequent injuries may face higher premiums. A clean bill of health often translates to lower costs.

  7. Policy Features and Riders:

    Customize and Cover
    Additional policy enhancements or riders, such as cost-of-living adjustments or return-of-premium features, can affect your premiums. The more features, the higher the cost.

  8. Geographic Location:

    Where You Plant Your Roots
    Living in an area with high medical costs or where certain disabilities are more prevalent can influence premium rates.

The Two Main Types of Disability Insurance

  1. Short-Term Disability Insurance (STDI)

    For The Immediate Needs
    Duration: Typically up to 3-6 months.
    Coverage: Usually 60-70% of the insured's base salary.
    Ideal For: Temporary illnesses, injuries, or maternity leaves. Ensures a seamless transition back to regular life without financial hiccups.

  2. Long-Term Disability Insurance (LTDI)

    The Long Haul Protector
    Duration: From a few years to retirement age.
    Coverage: Generally about 50-60% of the insured's base salary.
    Ideal For: Chronic illnesses, severe accidents, or conditions that require prolonged recovery periods. Offers peace of mind for extended challenges.

Disability Insurance Alternatives

Exploring Every Avenue

Insurance is about ensuring security, but it's not a one-size-fits-all solution. Sometimes, you need alternatives. At Leverage, we're not just experts in insurance, but in holistic financial protection.

  1. Emergency Savings Fund:

    A cushion of 3-6 months' worth of expenses can offer short-term relief in case of unforeseen events.

  2. Personal Accident Insurance:

    Specifically designed for accidents, it covers medical costs and offers a payout in case of permanent disablement.

  3. Critical Illness Coverage:

    A lump sum payment in the event of a serious illness diagnosis. Think of it as a financial safety net for the most challenging times.

  4. Workers' Compensation:

    For those workplace-related injuries, it provides wage replacement and medical benefits.
